# üéÆ Game Mechanics & Modules Guide

This document details the gamification mechanics and gameplay loops within the TOSBA platform. 
Our goal is to create a seamless **Learn-to-Earn** ecosystem where educational progress translates into creative freedom.

---

## üåü The Core Loop: Learn -> Earn -> Create

1.  **LEARN**: The child completes educational modules in the Curriculum.
2.  **EARN**: Success rewards the child with **Stars** ‚≠ê and **Items** üéí.
3.  **CREATE**: Stars and Items are used to craft new toys, art, and stories.

---

## üó∫Ô∏è Curriculum Map (The Journey)

The heart of the application. A dynamic, personalized path for the child.

*   **Progression**: The map is divided into Daily Missions (Monday - Friday).
*   **Modules**: Each day contains 3-5 specific learning modules (e.g., Math, Reading, Social Skills).
*   **Active State**: Only the current module is unlocked (pulsing). Future modules are locked üîí.
*   **Reward**: 
    *   Completing a question: +1 Star ‚≠ê
    *   Completing a full module: **Module Badge** + Bonus Stars + **Random Item Drop** üéÅ.

---

## üéí Inventory (My Bag)

Where the child stores their hard-earned loot.

*   **Items**: Stickers, toy parts, curious objects (e.g., "Dinosaur Bone", "Rocket Fin", "Shiny Gem").
*   **Origin**: Items are dynamically generated by Gemini 3 based on the child's **Interest Profile**.
    *   *If child likes Space:* Items will be "Moon Rock", "Alien Slime".
    *   *If child likes Nature:* Items will be "Rare Leaf", "Acorn".

---

## üõí The Shop (Marketplace)

A safe, simulated economy to teach financial literacy and decision making.

*   **Currency**: Uses virtual **Cash ($1 and $5 bills)** üíµ.
    *   **Finite Resource**: The child starts with a limited budget (e.g., $20).
    *   **Regeneration**: Cash is replenished after a **Cooldown Period** (e.g., every 24 hours or after completing a major milestone). This teaches patience and budgeting.
*   **Products**: 
    *   **Creative Tools**: "Rainbow Paintbrush" for Art Studio.
    *   **Blueprint Parts**: Components needed for the Fusion Workshop.
*   **Gameplay**: 
    *   Select item -> Check Price -> Pay Cash -> Item moves to Inventory.
    *   **Budgeting Lesson**: "I have $5, but the toy costs $7. I need to wait or save more."
    *   Teaches: Saving, spending, value, and delayed gratification.

## üîß Fusion Workshop (Crafting System)

Here, the child combines raw items to build complex "Super Toys".

*   **Blueprints**: The child selects a project (e.g., "Robo-T-Rex").
*   **Requirements**: The blueprint requires specific parts (e.g., 1x Metal Head, 2x Robo Legs).
*   **Fusion Process**: 
    *   Drag & Drop parts from Inventory into slots.
    *   Hit the **FUSE** button ‚ö°.
    *   **Gemini 3 nano banana** generates a brand new, unique image of the combined toy.
*   **Reward**: The new "Super Toy" is added to the sleek **Toy Shelf**.

## üìñ My Stories (Dynamic Library)

The child becomes the author of their own adventures.

*   **The Pot**: A magical cauldron in the UI.
*   **Input**: Child drags 2-3 items from their Inventory into the Pot (e.g., "Moon Rock" + "Teddy Bear").
*   **AI Action**: Gemini 3 writes a short, illustrated story involving those specific items.
    *   *Title*: "Teddy's Trip to the Moon".
*   **Output**: A flip-book style story appears in the library. The **Buddy** reads it aloud using TTS.

## üìö Magic Homework Converter (Worksheet Gamification)

Transforms boring paper worksheets into exciting, interactive games.

*   **Setup (Parent's Role)**:
    1.  **Information Source**: Parent takes a photo of a textbook page, worksheet, or teacher's note.
    2.  **Gemini 3 Vision** analyzes the content (e.g., "Single-digit addition problems" or "Biology: Parts of a Plant").
    3.  **Adaptation Engine**: The system selects the best Game Template for the content (e.g., 'Drag & Drop' for matching, 'Choice' for quiz).
    4.  **Personalization**: It generates new, age-appropriate visuals based on the child's interest (e.g., counting *Dinosaurs* instead of *Apples*).
    5.  **Review**: Parent reviews the generated game. If approved, it's added to the **Curriculum**.
*   **Gameplay (Child's Role)**:
    *   The child plays a fun game that reinforces the exact same concepts as their homework.
    *   **Goal**: Understand the concept through play, reducing homework stress.
*   **Reward**: Completion marks the homework as "Done" and awards Stars ‚≠ê.

## üé®üñºÔ∏è Scratch Reveal (Mystery Puzzle)

A sensory-friendly reward game.

*   **Trigger**: Often used as a "Break" or "Bonus" after a tough lesson.
*   **Gameplay**: 
    *   A grey/silver overlay hides an image.
    *   Child uses finger/mouse to "scratch" off the layer.
    *   As they scratch, a hidden image (related to their interest) is revealed.
*   **Collection**: The revealed image becomes a digital sticker in their album.

---

## üß© Puzzle Master

*   **Source**: Uses images from the **Scratch Reveal** or **Magic Art** sessions.
*   **Gameplay**: 
    *   The image is broken into jigsaw pieces (4, 9, or 16 pieces based on difficulty).
    *   Drag & Drop pieces to reassemble the memory.
*   **Skill**: Spatial awareness and fine motor skills.

## üè† AR-Lite Photo Hunt (Home Gallery)

Leveraging the child's familiar environment for learning.

*   **Setup (Parent's Role)**:
    1.  Parent takes a photo of a room (e.g., open fridge, messy bedroom, toy shelf).
    2.  **Gemini 3 Vision** analyzes the photo and automatically tags all identifiable objects (e.g., "Egg", "Milk", "Red Truck").
    3.  Parent reviews the tags and adds the photo to the **Curriculum** as a "Photo Hunt" module.
*   **Gameplay (Child's Role)**:
    *   The game displays the photo of *their actual home*.
    *   **Mission**: "Find the [LEMON]!" üçã
    *   The child taps on the lemon in the photo.
*   **Benefits**: 
    *   **Contextual Learning**: Learning happens in a familiar environment, not abstract clip-art.
    *   **Generalization**: Helps the child identify objects in the real world.
*   **Reward**: High Star value ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê.
